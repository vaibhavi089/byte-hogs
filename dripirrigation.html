What is Drip Irrigation?

Drip irrigation is a highly efficient method of delivering water directly to the plant’s roots, minimizing water wastage and improving crop yield. It is an eco-friendly, cost-effective way to irrigate large fields as well as small gardens.

Benefits of Drip Irrigation

Water Efficiency

Reduces water wastage by delivering water directly to the plant’s root zone, reducing evaporation and runoff.

Increased Crop Yield

By maintaining soil moisture at optimal levels, crops grow healthier and more productive.

Soil Health

Prevents soil erosion and nutrient leaching, keeping the soil fertile and healthy for a longer time.

How Does Drip Irrigation Work?

Drip irrigation works by slowly releasing water through emitters placed along irrigation tubing. The emitters can be adjusted to deliver the right amount of water directly to the base of the plants.

Drip Irrigation Process

Water Source

The system is connected to a water source, such as a tank or well, ensuring a constant supply.

Pipes & Tubing

Water flows through pipes and tubes that are laid along the rows of plants.

Emitters

Emitters control the flow of water and release it directly at the plant's root zone.
