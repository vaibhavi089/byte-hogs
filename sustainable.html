What is Sustainable Farming?

Sustainable farming refers to agricultural practices that meet current food needs without compromising the ability of future generations to meet their needs. It encompasses a wide range of methods including organic farming, agroecology, and regenerative agriculture.

Benefits of Sustainable Farming
  • Reduces environmental impact
  • Improves soil health
  • Promotes biodiversity
  • Enhances food security
  • Supports local economies
Best Practices for Sustainable Farming
1. Crop Rotation

Changing the types of crops grown in a specific area each season helps improve soil health and reduce pests.

2. Cover Cropping

Using cover crops can prevent soil erosion, improve soil health, and enhance biodiversity.

3. Integrated Pest Management (IPM)

IPM involves using a combination of biological, cultural, physical, and chemical methods to manage pests in an environmentally sensitive way.

4. Conservation Tillage

This practice minimizes soil disturbance, which helps maintain soil structure and reduce erosion.
